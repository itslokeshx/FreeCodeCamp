The opportunity cost of 337 hours.

337h 6m of pure Chrome entertainment. Here's what that same time, redirected, would have looked like.

8x your coding output

337h ÷ 43h 57m = 7.66

You could have 3-4 finished full-stack apps in your portfolio by now. Each one a stepping stone.

43x ESIOT's study time

337h ÷ 7h 48m = 43.2

You could have mastered every subject end-to-end and still had time to read 4–5 books cover-to-cover.

~3 months of 4h gym days

337h ÷ 2h = 168 workouts

Two-a-days for 12 weeks. The body is a system, and right now yours is running at maintenance.

The honest verdict.

From January through March, you were not in control. Your phone was. The numbers are not kind — 7–13 hours of mindless scrolling for every 1 hour of real work. If this case study were a job performance review, the rating would be: Needs Improvement.

"But the second half of this story is genuinely remarkable."

From April onward, you did three things right: (1) you tracked yourself honestly, (2) you let the data confront you, and (3) you didn't quit when the chart was ugly. June is the cleanest month — focus outpacing drain, habit tier unlocked, Chrome down 84% from January.

The trap now is believing the work is done. It's not. 337 hours of waste still sits on the 2026 scoreboard. The next 337 hours are being written right now — make sure the next monthly report looks like June, not February.

Methodology note. Phone usage from YourHour monthly PDF reports (6 reports, Jan 1 – Jun 11 2026).
